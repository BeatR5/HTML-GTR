GTR R34 Information

The technology and performance of the R32 GT-R prompted the Australian motoring publication Wheels to nickname the GT-R "Godzilla" in its July 1989 edition.[1][2] Wheels then carried the name through all the generations of Skyline GT-Rs, most notably the R34 GT-R, which they nicknamed "Godzilla Returns", and described as "The best handling car we have ever driven". In tests conducted by automotive publications, R34 GT-R have covered a quarter of a mile (402 metres) in 12.2 seconds from a standing start time and accelerated from 0–100 km/h (0–62 mph) in 4.4 seconds.

Nissan Skyline GTR R34

The Nissan Skyline GT-R R34 is an iconic Japanese sports car celebrated for its performance and distinctive design. Produced from 1999 to 2002, it's revered for its powerful twin-turbocharged RB26DETT engine, all-wheel drive system, and advanced technology for its time, like the ATTESA E-TS Pro system. Its appearances in popular media, including movies and video games, have solidified its status as a cult classic among automotive enthusiasts worldwide.
